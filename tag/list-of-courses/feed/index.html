]]> Many of you must have wondered what the finest job prospects are after 12th Arts. Thousands of students complete their higher secondary exams i.e.  12th grade in the arts stream each year. And the one question that arises in their mind after finishing  board examinations is, “What do I do after 12th arts?” What are the best career prospects for arts students?

Arts (or humanities) as a subject of study is inspired by history, language, cultures, societal norms, literature, visual arts, music, and other creative aspects of existence. Students studying the arts are taught and prepared to use their exploratory, logical, and critical thinking skills.

History, geography, sociology, economics, mass communication, law, psychology, political science, linguistics, and language studies are some of the most appealing humanities courses (English, Hindi, Sanskrit, etc.). One of the best aspects of 12th arts is that there are numerous professional opportunities available after 12th arts. To choose the correct job alternatives for art students, one must be aware of all career options accessible after the 12th-grade arts, which can be overwhelming due to a large number of choices available. In this blog, we’ll provide you with a list of courses post 12th-grade arts.

BACHELORS OF ARTS (B.A.)

A B.a. is a three-year full-time undergraduate course in three domains: humanities, social sciences, and liberal arts. It is accessible in a variety of specializations, including Psychology, History, Archaeology, Geography, Economics, Journalism, Mass Communication, Sociology, Anthropology, Literature (all major languages), and Environmental Science, to mention a few. As part of this undergraduate curriculum, students must choose a major (honors subject) as well as a few electives. BA is among the most preferred career paths for students of the arts.

BACHELOR OF FINE ARTS

If you’re thinking about what after 12th arts, studying fine arts is a common choice of career among individuals. Bachelor of Fine Arts is an excellent professional path for creative individuals. B.F.A. is a three-year degree that encompasses Fine Arts, Visual Arts, and Performing Arts, just like the B.A. degree. Theater, Film Making, Music, Dance, Painting & Sculpting, Pottery & Ceramics, Creative Writing, Architectural Drawing, Textile Design, and Animation are just a few of the distinct specialist courses available to students.

This undergraduate degree provides a myriad of diverse employment opportunities. Teachers/Professors, Creative Writers, Professional Dancers/Singers/Musicians, Art Directors, Theatre/Drama Actors, and a variety of other careers are available to B.F.A graduates. Today, a Fine Arts education is connected with distinction and class, and it is regarded as one of the top employment prospects for students of the arts.

BACHELOR OF BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

BBA is perhaps the most renowned course offered to students and one of the greatest courses available after 12th Arts. Bachelor of Business Administration is the most sought-after and studied bachelor degree career option after graduating from high school. The finest feature about the B.B.A. program is that it is open to students from all three streams: arts, commerce, and science. It is a three-year, full-time undergraduate program that leads to employment prospects in a variety of industries such as sales, marketing, finance, education, hospitality, and government. BBA is a prominent professional path for arts majors.HR Management, Sales & Marketing, Finance, and Information Technology are some of the most common specialties offered in B.B.A. courses. Students can pursue the B.B.A. degree full-time or via correspondence, and it is regarded as one of the greatest arts employment alternatives.

Students with a B.B.A. degree can pursue high-level positions such as HR Manager/HR Executive, Marketing Manager/Marketing Executive, Sales Manager/Sales Executive, and R&D Executive. Because of the benefits and rewards, BBA is one of the most popular courses after 12th arts.

B.B.A+L.L.B – INTEGRATED LAW COURSE

As the name implies, an integrated legal study consists of two degrees: a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) and a Bachelor of Legislative Law (L.L.B.). The course lasts 5 years, consisting of ten semesters because it integrates two full-fledged courses. The most significant advantage of studying an integrated law course is the ability to achieve dual degrees within the framework of a single curriculum. It is the finest job option for kids who are interested in law with an arts background.

A bachelor’s degree is usually required to pursue an L.L.B. degree. However, if you choose an integrated law program, you can begin studying law immediately after graduating from high school. The Indian Bar Council wholeheartedly supports integrated legal programs. As a result, students with a B.A. + L.L.B. degree can appear for the Bar Council of India test to receive a professional license to practice law, making it one of the top courses after 12th arts.

BACHELOR OF JOURNALISM AND MASS COMMUNICATION  (B.J.M)

A bachelor’s degree in journalism and mass communication is a three-year program separated into six semesters. Print Journalism, Television and Radio Production, Television Production and Writing, Advertising and Public Relations, Event Management, Social Media, Film Studies, and Media Law and Ethics are among the topics covered in the course program.

The wide course curriculum is properly balanced to enable students to learn technical knowledge in all key sectors of journalism and mass communication while also imparting theoretical information governing their aspects and application.

After 12th arts, BJM is one of the most popular courses. A B.J.M. graduate can work in radio, print media, journalism (both print and digital), television production, films, marketing and advertising, and public relations, among other fields.

BACHELOR OF FASHION DESIGN (B.F.D)

Bachelor of Fashion Design is a three to a four-year undergraduate program intended specifically for those who want to work in the fashion industry. The B.F.D. curriculum incorporates contemporary fashion industry trends and helps students learn how to produce creative ideas for clothing, jewellery, footwear, and accessories, among other things.

Students are not only exposed to the ever-changing fashion industry but they are also taught how to use various creative and analytical techniques/tools to create items based on market trends and client wants, making it one of the top courses available after 12th arts.

After earning a B.F.D., students can seek employment (apprenticeships) with prominent fashion businesses and designers in the country.

BACHELOR OF HOTEL MANAGEMENT (B.H.M)

Bachelor of Hospitality Management is a three to four-year undergraduate management program that prepares students to work in the hospitality business. Tourism, event management, housing management, and the food and beverage business are among the specialist areas covered in the course.

After completing the B.H.M. program, individuals can pursue a variety of positions such as Hotel Manager, Accommodation Manager, Meeting & Event Planner, Food Service Manager, Sales Manager, Food and Beverage Manager, Catering Officer, and so on.
