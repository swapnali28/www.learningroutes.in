The Emerging Field of Business Analytics

Business Analytics refers to the study of analysis and interpretation of a company’s data to prepare an optimum business strategy for them. It focuses exclusively on analytics and is suitable for young minds who have an aptitude for both information technology and business management. Companies that are totally reliant on data treat it as an asset, thus, they look for skilled professionals who can deal with it efficiently. 

Scope of Business Analytics

For any company, it is utmost important to stay relevant and ahead of its competitors. So, they constantly innovate their business. They ditch old, time-consuming methods and innovate better ones to meet their business demands. As methods are innovated, the amount of data for companies pile up. This is where business analytics step in. If a company’s data is handled efficiently, that company is bound to flourish. Analytics help  to make informed business decisions.

According to a study by Analytics India Industry, the industry in  India is increasing at a considerable rate of 23.8% per year.  BBA in Business Analytics helps a student to acquire the skill-set like statistical analysis, data mining, forecasting, text analytics, data visualisation and much more to read data and strategise into a business plan for the companys’ success. 

NMIMS Global BBA in Business Analytics 

NMIMS offers a 3-year BBA in Business Analytics in Open and Distance Learning Mode. The program is specially designed to equip students with the concept of business management, data analytics and data visualisation across all functional domains of a company. The program offers a perfect blend of academics and industry-specific knowledge that ensures that the students get real-time experience. It gives hands-on experience to its students to teach them to deal with both structured and unstructured data. It is designed to equip students to become managers and business analysts at entry level positions. 

NMIMS Global BBA in Business Analytics ( Distance Mode) uses real-world case studies to teach business concepts and prepares its students to use analytical tools like Python, Excel and Tableau. The course is spread over 6 semesters with 6 subjects taught in every semester. The program is packed with modules in soft-skills, technical skills and business management skills.

Features and Outcomes 

Employability Skills

NMIMS has added a unique module to its program structure which lays special emphasis on developing students’ employability skills by honing their soft and technical skills. It has added the module ‘Soft Skills for Managers’ in the first semester. Similarly, it has added modules like ‘Design Thinking’ and ‘Start Your Start Up’ in the second and third semester respectively which helps students execute the knowledge they have gained during the course. 

Scope after BBA in Business Analytics from NMIMS

A student graduating in BBA Business Analytics from NMIMS can work as an Assistant Business Analyst working on development of business models for companies. He/she works with cross-functional teams, to analyse or generate accurate business forecasts. 

He/she can also work as Assistant Project Manager to assist a company in planning, management and implementation of business projects. He/she can also work in the sphere of data collection as a Business Intelligence Associate to assist the team in data collection and to identify data anomalies.

]]> With an MBA in Business Analytics, you can pursue a variety of job paths. As business analytics has become an increasingly important element of an organization’s everyday operations, career possibilities in this field are expanding. As a result of the increased need for MBA graduates with analytical skills, analytics has emerged as a relatively new and popular management specialization.

Options for a Career After completing an MBA in Business Analytics

The quantity of data we have at our disposal is continually expanding, and making sense of it in order to derive useful insights and interpretations has become increasingly vital. This is where the field of business analytics comes into play. Business analytics is the study of large data using analytics, statistics, predictive analytics, and other optimization tools and approaches to convey insights to relevant stakeholders.

Business analysts may assist a firm in making critical choices such as cost reduction, customer happiness, and profit enhancement. Let’s look at the many employment choices available after completing an MBA in Business Analytics program.

MBA in Business Analytics: What Does It Entail?

Because the field of analytics is expanding, combining a management degree with the acquisition of analytical abilities can help you develop your profession. An MBA in Business Analytics will provide you with the following abilities:

  • Database design
  • Python and R
  • Survey/query software
  • SQL databases 
  • Data visualisation
  • Effective Communication
  • Creative Thinking
  • Business intelligence and reporting software
  • Problem-solving skills
  • Industry Knowledge

You must hold a graduate degree in any discipline and pass the national eligibility examination for an MBA program to be eligible for this course. After finishing your course, you will be able to enter one of three primary domains. Descriptive analysis, prescriptive analysis, and predictive analysis are the three types of analysis. Companies assign you to a position based on your qualifications, subject knowledge, and job experience. Every sector, from multinational corporations to e-commerce businesses, is eager to acquire an MBA with analytical abilities since it is the way of the future.

1. Data Analyst

A data analyst is a person who collects and analyses data based on sales figures, market research, logistics, and other factors. They use their technical knowledge to assure data quality and strive toward correctly processing and presenting it. As a result, the company is able to make informed business decisions.

The day-to-day responsibilities of a data analyst vary based on the business or speciality in question. To identify corporate goals, data analysts are frequently needed to collaborate with data scientists, IT teams, and management. The following are some of the most prevalent talents needed to work as a data analyst:

  • Database Querying Languages
  • Creative and Analytical Thinking
  • Data Visualization
  • Programming Languages (R/SAS)
  • Effective Communication
  • SQL Databases
  • Machine Learning
  • Advanced Microsoft Excel
  • Data Mining, Cleaning and Munging

2. Supply Chain Analyst 

Improving an operation’s performance is one of a supply chain analyst’s main tasks. They accomplish this by determining the needs for a specific project and coordinating them with the rest of the team. Engineers and quality assurance specialists are required to test their new supply chain procedures, thus a supply chain analyst presents the facts to them. He or she also makes suggestions for how to improve performance, cut expenses, and keep track of inventories.

In India, the average income for a Supply Chain Analyst is 469,747 rupees.

3. Business Analyst 

Understanding changing business demands, assessing the effect of these changes, defining and analyzing requirements, and documenting and fostering communication among all stakeholders are all things that business analysts accomplish. In India, the national average income for a Business Analyst is $66,084. Over the last five years, it is believed that about 90% of all businesses have used analytics in their operations. And that this industry has witnessed an increase in prospects in practically every discipline. Business Analytics has become a standard technique in the IT and corporate sectors.

The following are some of the skills needed to work as a Business Analyst:

  1. Survey/query software
  2. Statistical analysis software (e.g. R, SAS, SPSS, or STATA)
  3. Python and R
  4. SQL databases and database querying languages
  5. Data visualisation
  6. Database design
  7. Business intelligence 
  8. Data mining

4. Technical Team Lead

A technical team lead is a professional who is in charge of leading a technical team in any organization. Their primary responsibility is to guarantee that technological development is appropriately addressed, that products are of good quality, and that they are delivered on schedule. They’d need technical talents, as well as strong leadership and management abilities.

The typical annual pay is between 4 and 8 lakhs.

5. Big data Analyst

Importing, cleaning, covering, and analyzing data are all tasks that the big data analyst must assist with in order to get insights and make judgments.

6. Business Intelligent Expert

The Business Intelligent Expert is in charge of the business organization’s design, development, implementation, administration, and support mission.
