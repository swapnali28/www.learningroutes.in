We can begin with the Basic Introduction 

NMIMS– Established in 1981, NMIMS has now emerged as a globally reputed university. It was initiated by Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal (SVKM) to meet the country’s growing demand for management institutes. His efforts led to the creation of the Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies (NMIMS). From a very basic start, today NMIMS is a Deemed to be University offering multiple disciplines across multiple campuses. The Institute has 17 specialized schools, 17000 students, and about 750 full-time faculty members.NMIMS has established itself as one of the nation’s premier centres of educational excellence and research due to its consistent academic quality, research concentration, faculty from top national and international institutes, and strong industry ties.

Amity University – Amity University in Noida, Uttar Pradesh, established in 2005, is a worldwide education leader with a system that integrates the best methods, standards, ideas, and resources from around the world. In India, Amity has become a notable name. Association of Indian Universities and the Association of Commonwealth Universities are both members of Amity. Amity has attracted over 80,000 students for various programs thanks to affiliations with UGC, NAAC, NATA, and other organizations. The course offerings and fee structure at Amity are well-known.

Distance BBA course

Distance BBA courses is a popular alternative to full-time BBA courses for students who want to study the program without attending classes in person or while working part-time or full-time. There are many governments or state colleges known for offering UGC-approved or AICTE-accredited education programs

Students must have at least a passing grade in class 10+2 or a diploma after class 10 or class 12 to be considered for admission. For admission to BBA programs, some universities (IGNOU, TNOU Chennai) hold entrance exams. The average cost of a distance education course is INR 3,000 to 30,000.

Students can pursue an MBA or PGDM after completing their Bachelors in Business Administration, either full-time or through distance education. The average income offered to 23 candidates after graduation is INR 2-5 LPA, with skill and job experience likely to improve.

Top Colleges for pursuing Long-distance BBA 

1. NMIMS

The NMIMS Global Access School For Continuing Education is India’s leading remote learning institution. In India, the college provides a variety of undergraduate and postgraduate management programs. One of NMIMS’ most popular courses in the Bachelor of Business administration (BBA).

Students who have completed their 10+2 can enroll in this three-year degree program.

The NMIMS Global distance BBA program is designed for students who desire to pursue a career in business administration. Students might pursue an MBA or a diploma program in management for higher education after completing the program. The three-year program prepares students to be industry-ready managers. NMIMS offers a 6-semester program. The program is very flexible and convenient.

Eligibility requirement- A 10+2 diploma from a recognized board with a minimum of 50% marks. The AICTE has authorized a 10th grader with a three-year diploma.

The Admissions Procedure-Students must complete an online admission form to be considered for admission. Students must upload appropriate documents and pay the admission cost after completing the application form.

After verification, NMIMS will assign the student a student number.

NMIMS Distance BBA  Fees – The course fee is INR 25000 every semester.

2. Amity University 

Amity has written its significance in the last few decades, with campuses across the globe. The university holds more patents than any other private university, and its student body is diverse. Since its inception, Amity has successfully placed over 30,000 students and continues to impress.

Eligibility:

Candidates must have finished formal schooling and have valid certifications in grades 10 and 10+2.

The application procedure is as follows:

Candidates can apply either online or in-person at any recognized study center. The university will conduct a merit screening.

Structure of Fees: The cost of the three-year program is Rs. 1.22 lakh.

 3. Suresh Gyan Vihar University 

In a short period, the university received NAAC Grade “A” accreditation. The university, which is known for its safe and all-in-one distance learning courses, ensures that its students have a higher chance of finding work. Students pursuing distant learning courses can also make use of app-based learning opportunities.

Eligibility: A minimum of a grade of 10 and a grade of 10+2 from a recognized school or college are required.

Candidates can either fill out the application form manually or submit it electronically. Other relevant certifications can also be submitted to the university online or by mail.

Candidates can pay the entire fee of 72,000 INR. Otherwise, a deposit of 26,400 INR is required at the time of admission. The fee per semester is 13,200 INR.

4. Annamalai University 

Annamalai University, one of India’s oldest, is well-known for its traditional teaching methods and strong adherence to discipline. Thanks to decades of exceptional education investment in students, the university’s placement record remains unblemished.

Eligibility: A basic requirement of a student to pass 10 and 10+2 with a good grade.

Candidates can apply online through the university’s website or directly through authorized study centers located throughout the country. The prospectus charge is approximately 150 Indian rupees.

Fee Structure: A 22,500 INR annual payment would be required. Another 1500 INR is required for study materials, which must be paid annually.

LPU, regarded as one of the most wealthy and fastly growing universities in India, has resisted the stereotype of private institution eligibility: The university has a very basic eligibility requirement, requiring merely 10 and 10+2.
