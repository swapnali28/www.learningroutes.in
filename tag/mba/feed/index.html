]]> For freshers aspiring to become entrepreneurs, pursuing an MBA can be a valuable tool in charting their path towards success. While embarking on the journey of entrepreneurship can be thrilling, it can also be a daunting prospect. In such circumstances, the advantages of an MBA programme can be instrumental in simplifying the process of becoming an entrepreneur. 

By equipping individuals with skills like business planning, marketing, strategy, and finance, an MBA provides them with the knowledge and tools they need to make critical decisions and run a successful venture.

While an MBA is not a mandatory requirement for success as an entrepreneur, it can offer several benefits. It helps individuals gain a deeper understanding of market trends, customer needs, and the overall business environment, allowing them to make more informed decisions. 

Additionally, an MBA can provide individuals with a competitive edge over their peers, offering them the necessary skills and knowledge to succeed in a highly competitive business landscape.

6 Ways an MBA Can Make You a Better Entrepreneur

Running a business is a challenging journey that involves uncertainty, ambiguity, and risk. Navigating the business world can be difficult if you don’t have a solid plan in mind. 

However, pursuing an MBA degree can provide you with a safe and reliable path to becoming a successful entrepreneur. In addition, an MBA programme can also provide you with the relevant knowledge and skills to manage a business effectively.

If you are confused, here are several ways in which an MBA degree can help individuals become better entrepreneurs:

1. Learning Diverse Business Skills

As an entrepreneur, you will often find yourself juggling between various roles, such as CEO, Accountant, Marketer, and more. This is especially true for small business owners with limited resources to hire additional staff. For such individuals, pursuing an MBA can be a valuable way to develop a broad range of business skills to help them succeed in any role.

As an MBA programme covers various subjects, from accounting and finance to marketing and operations. Through the programme, you will learn various important skills including business strategy, organisational behaviour, and economics. 

Additionally, you will also get a chance to develop essential skills like negotiation, public speaking, and teamwork. If you have a particular interest or passion, such as global management, you can specialise in that by pursuing an on-campus or online MBA programme in that field.

2. Fostering Global Perspective

With the rising popularity of business schools globally, pursuing an MBA degree offers an excellent opportunity to meet and interact with people from diverse cultural backgrounds. This can broaden one’s perspectives and foster a global mindset, essential in today’s interconnected world.

In addition, a global mindset also offers personal growth and financial benefits to organisations. According to a study, companies with an international outlook tend to perform better financially. In addition, they are better equipped to navigate cultural differences and build relationships with stakeholders worldwide.

As per World Economic Forum, by the year 2025, most of the global workforce will comprise millennials and Generation Z. As these generations value diversity and inclusion; therefore, businesses that prioritise these values can attract and retain top talent.

3. Learning Your Way

If you are an entrepreneur who values independence and innovation, pursuing an MBA could be a perfect choice. MBA programmes are designed to be flexible and accessible for busy working professionals, with online options available at affordable prices.

One of the key benefits of an MBA programme is the freedom and autonomy it provides. With a wide range of elective courses and opportunities to tailor your education to your unique interests and goals, you can focus on the areas of business that interest you the most.

Lastly, by enrolling in an MBA programme, you can combine your independent spirit with the knowledge and skills you need to succeed in business. So if you’re looking for a way to take your entrepreneurial ambitions to the next level, an MBA could be just what you need.

4. Expanding Your Network

Entrepreneurs know the importance of networking. In fact, their success often hinges on the strength of their connections. A robust network can provide fresh ideas, perspectives, and insights into new products, technologies, and business growth strategies.

An MBA programme offers access to like-minded and ambitious individuals who share your passion for business. Your classmates and professors can be valuable resources and sounding boards for ideas. 

In addition, MBA programmes also organise networking events and career fairs, which enable students to connect with business leaders and potential employers. These events not only allow you to expand your network but also stay up-to-date with the latest industry trends and practices.

5. Obtaining Leadership Skills

Becoming a successful entrepreneur is not just about having great ideas; it also involves leading a team towards success. Unfortunately, not everyone is a born leader, and 77% of businesses report a lack of leadership in their organisation. This is where pursuing an MBA programme can be beneficial for business owners, as it emphasises interpersonal skills development, which is essential for effective leadership.

MBA programmes offer courses in management, communication, and organisational behaviour that can give students a deeper understanding of what makes a great leader. Additionally, MBA students can put their leadership skills into practice through various projects and internships. For example, they may lead a team to complete a consulting project for a client or take up a leadership role in a student-run club or organisation. These experiences allow them to apply their learning in real-world situations and gain valuable feedback and insights.

6. Learning Risk Management

An MBA programme can be a game-changer for entrepreneurs when it comes to managing risks and making informed decisions. In the unpredictable business world, risks are inevitable, but an MBA programme can equip you with the skills to assess and manage them effectively.

While some entrepreneurs may rely on intuition, an MBA programme emphasises the importance of informed decision-making based on market analysis and data-driven insights. You will learn to identify the right factors, make calculated investments, and make strategic moves to propel your business forward.

In short, pursuing an MBA can give you the superpowers you need to navigate the challenges of the business world with confidence and make decisions that will help you stay ahead of the competition.

Conclusion

Entrepreneurs wondering whether pursuing an MBA is worth it should know that the answer is a resounding “Yes.” The benefits that an MBA can provide, such as networking opportunities, exposure to diverse perspectives, leadership training, and access to experienced mentors, are invaluable for entrepreneurs as they pitch their ideas, plan new ventures, and develop strategies for funding and growth. In some cases, entrepreneurs may forge new paths and create business models for which no established benchmarks exist. As an entrepreneur with an MBA, you will be better equipped to navigate the challenges of breaking new ground and to overcome the naysayers who claim, “It has never been done that way before.”

Tips to Pick the MBA Specialisation For the Future

Now that you are familiar with the most sought-after MBA specialisations, let’s explore how to make an informed decision in selecting the ideal MBA programme for you. Here, we have listed a few things that you must keep in mind while selecting an MBA stream.

1. Career Goals

Deciding MBA in which stream is the best for you requires careful consideration of your career aspirations. It’s important to take into account the type of job you want after graduation and the industry that appeals to you. By aligning your specialisation with your career goals, you will be able to focus your studies and gain the skills and knowledge necessary to achieve success. Take the time to evaluate what you want to achieve in your career, and let that guide your choice of specialisation.

2. Industry Trends

Staying acquainted with industry trends is crucial when selecting which stream of MBA is the best for you. Understanding which industries are flourishing and which are experiencing a downturn is crucial to making an informed decision. Before settling on a specialisation, it is recommended to assess the prospects of your chosen industry to ensure that your investment in an MBA degree will yield desired results. Stay informed and keep up-to-date with the latest trends to make an informed choice.

3. Personal Interests

Personal Interests play a key role in determining your MBA specialisation. After all, you will be dedicating two years of study to your chosen subject. Hence, it is imperative to choose a specialisation that ignites your passion and interests. This not only makes the learning experience more enjoyable but also enhances your performance in your courses. Pursuing a specialisation that aligns with your interests ensures a fulfilling academic journey and sets you on the path towards fulfilling your career aspirations.

4. Skill Set

Assessing your current skill set is a crucial step in choosing the right MBA specialisation. Consider both the skills you possess and the skills you wish to develop through your MBA programme. Different specialisations place varying demands on technical proficiency, communication abilities, and leadership skills. By considering the skills you have and the skills you hope to acquire, you will be able to make a well-informed decision and select a specialisation that aligns with your personal and professional goals.

5. Job Availability

When choosing an MBA specialisation, it is important to consider the job market and the availability of the type of position you desire. Analyse the demand for your desired job in your region and the job prospects for your target industry in the coming years. By considering these factors, you will be able to make an informed choice and select a specialisation that leads to fulfilling career opportunities. Ensure that your investment in an MBA degree is supported by a robust job market and a bright future outlook for your desired career path.

6. Job Requirements

Having an understanding of the job requirements for the type of role you are aiming for post-graduation is essential in making an informed decision about your MBA specialisation. Having a specialised area of focus can be advantageous in increasing your chances of securing the job you want. Researching the job requirements and understanding the value of a specialised area of knowledge can help you make an educated decision about your MBA specialisation.

7. Salary Expectations

When considering what MBA specialisation to pursue, take into account your salary expectations. Research the average salaries for the type of job you hope to attain post-graduation and select a specialisation that can help you reach your financial objectives.

8. Networking Opportunities

Maximise the networking potential of your MBA specialisation by researching and attending professional organisations and events related to your desired industry. Networking is an invaluable tool for building relationships and getting your foot in the door when the time arrives for you to apply for jobs. Take advantage of your specialisation to make meaningful connections that may lead to a successful career.

9. Work-Life Balance

When deciding on an MBA specialisation, take into account the associated work-life balance that comes with it. Do your research to determine the hours required, and select a field that allows you to maintain a healthy balance between your professional and personal life.

10. Program Reputation

When researching an MBA programme, it is essential to consider its reputation within the industry you are interested in. You must consider looking into awards and accreditations before finalising a programme in a college. An excellent reputation can open doors for you in the future, so investigate whether the program has a positive standing among employers and if its alumni network is strong. A program with a robust alumni network can be especially beneficial, as it can provide invaluable insight into the job market and help you make crucial connections.

Final Thoughts

People pursue an MBA to expand their knowledge and bolster their professional opportunities. However, to ensure the best results, it’s essential to make the right choice when it comes to selecting a field of specialization. To ensure that you make an informed decision on which stream of MBA is the best for you, carefully consider each point discussed in this blog. These key points will guide you towards the best MBA program for you.

“Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.”

-Nelson Mandela 

Most Indian parents want their children to pursue a management degree due to the many perks it has to offer. But ever since globalisation has expanded its roots formal education has taken a back seat. Hence the students are in a dilemma whether to pursue a degree or not. Reports have also shown that a management degree is not a pre-requisite to land good jobs or higher positions in organisations. However, having an MBA in Finance, Banking or Marketing Management is always a bonus.

Is an MBA Degree Helpful for Managing Business?

India ranks 2 for having the highest number of MBA graduates in the world. No doubt, a management degree can enhance the way you perceive things. One in five MBA applicants, according to the 2019 CarringtonCrisp study of applicants from around the world, thought to enrol in a program to launch their own business. Additionally, entrepreneurship rose from tenth to fifth most popular alternatives in the MBA curriculum in only one year, making it one of the most valued specialisations. 

Skills that Formal Management Education Helps You Develop 

Management education is a popular course that is expected to have an increased ROI. Over the period of time, universities have focused on adding more specialisations in their management education curriculum. Not only that, the format of the education system has also changed.  With globalisation penetrating in India, online and distance education is given equal importance. The education system has made management curriculums more holistic, introducing practical skills through case studies, capstone projects and/or internship opportunities. Hence, the students get a chance to enhance their skills along with theoretical knowledge. 

Following are some skills that management education helps you develop: – 

  1. Position Your Brand in the National and International Market 
  2. Jack of all Trades – Finance, Accounting, Business Law, Entrepreneurship etc. 
  3. Teamwork and Leadership Skills 
  4. Build and Expand Network 
  5. Marketing Tactics 

And much more. 

Do CEOs of Fortune 500 Companies have Management Degrees? 

Studies have shown that one-third of the CEOs of Fortune 500 companies have a management degree. Here is a tabular presentation of the top 10 individuals who have a management degree and have swayed the world with their entrepreneurial skills. Not only Fortune 500 companies have a good number of MBA graduates but also others global brand names who have put their degree to use such as – Sheryl Sandberg, Elon Musk, Tim Cook, Meg Whitman and the list goes on.   

Should You Pursue Formal Education to Land a CEO Position? 

People are not born with the in-built skills of a CEO. They need assistance during their education to inculcate the right skills. A management degree can accelerate this process. Also, if you do your course from a recognized business school, considering the placement opportunities, networking opportunities, curriculum, etc., can also boost your business mindset development. In India, some of the top B-Schools are IIMs, Narsee Monjee Institute of Management, Amity University, Chandigarh University among others

Which Course Should You Choose to Become a CEO? 

So far we have discovered the importance of a management degree. Now, let’s take this conversation to the next level. If you think about it, you would want to pick an MBA if you want an up and running successful business because of many reasons. But Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies and other prestigious universities in India also offers a course called “Executive MBA” 

According to Coursera “An Executive MBA (EMBA) is an advanced business degree program designed for professionals who are about 10 to 15 years into their careers.” 

NMIMS’ executive MBA is a 24-month course that is thoughtfully crafted and packed with skill-based knowledge. The university offers five specialisations in this course, namely: – 

  1. Marketing
  2. Digital Marketing
  3. Applied Finance
  4. Operations and Supply Chain
  5. Leadership and Strategy

Moreover, they have collaborated with Harvard Business School  to provide an optional certificate course for value addition, thus, exposing students to the global education system. 

Is Executive MBA Better than MBA?

You must be wondering why an Executive MBA is better than an MBA. There are a lot of similarities in the course such as duration, application process, test requirements, etc. but EMBA has an edge because the individual already has 10 to 12 years of corporate experience so as to build something of his own. 

According to Caryn L. Beck-Dudley, the president and CEO of AACSB International, the enrollment in executive courses has increased to 68%. This degree is meant for working professionals who want to land leadership positions in organisations. It is an ideal way to give a push to your career if you are at a standstill. 

Final Thoughts 

We are sure you can come up with many reasons and examples saying a management degree is not necessary but we are saying that an MBA or an Executive MBA can help accelerate the process. You get to build a network, learn how theoretical knowledge can be executed to practical projects. Through the capstone projects and internships students learn how to work in a team which is imperative to build a business.
