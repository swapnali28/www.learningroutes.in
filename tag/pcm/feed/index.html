ENGINEERING

This is one of the most difficult careers in the non-medical science streams. Some students hope to achieve a high score in order to pursue a career as an engineer. To continue with engineering studies, a student can pursue a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) or a Bachelor of Engineering (B.E).

ARCHITECTURE

This B. Arch is the second-highest career option for scientific students after engineering. The course teaches the study of design, procedure, building construction, and building structures. This field is a work of art and ideas to make them a reality in the form of houses, buildings, bridges, and so on.

A degree course entails the incorporation of artwork into mathematical and scientific calculations in order to develop the entire framework of a specific project. Candidates can also pursue higher education for postgraduate programs such as Masters in Architecture or Masters in Designing, among others. Architecture Designer, Interior Designer, Architecture Draftsman, Project Assistant Manager, Architecture Engineer, and others are the positions that a B.Arch degree holder can apply for.

PILOT

Those who want to fly high into the skies may consider this career. It is ideal for students who do not need to work a 9 to 5 job. This career not only allows you to fly high, but it also offers you the wings to reach your goals. This job combines science, mathematics, and technology with the opportunity to travel to various sites, cities, and even abroad. A pilot’s job is not easy; it is full of responsibility that a pilot must carry on their strong shoulders in order to properly take off and land the flight.

To become a pilot candidate, candidates must pass various levels of selection, beginning with a written test and progressing to a physical test.

MERCHANT NAVY

Merchant Navy is a career that runs in the seas or oceans to convey products, services, and trade to various locations throughout the world via ships. It is one of the several techniques to trade in different nations or across seas. Merchant ships employ a large number of people to transport supplies, machinery, and freight all around the world.

The merchant navy officers remain on the ship until the products and services are delivered. Candidates interested in working in the Merchant Navy can become deck officers, radio officers, engineers, chief engineering officers, and so on. Students who want to become merchant officers must have a high percentage in their scientific stream with PCM as their main subjects.

NATIONAL DEFENSE SERVICES

If students have a strong desire to help others, they should put their service to their motherland first. National defense services provide significant and well-respected job possibilities. An aspirant can join one of three national defense services: the Indian Army, the Indian Navy, or the Indian Air Force.
