What is Operations Management?

Operations Management fundamentally deals with the evolution, development, and delivery of products and services. This two-year postgraduate programme focuses on all the aspects of business processes, covering the whole life cycle of a product or service.  It offers theoretical frameworks and analytical tools to enhance significant choices made during the planning and management of the entire operational process. 

The various topics covered under the curriculum are Managerial Economics, Demand & Business Forecasting, Quantitative Techniques & Methods, Enterprise Resource Planning, Production Planning & Control, Material Management; and many more relating to the development of products and services. 

They provide you with many future job opportunities which will help you to build a promising career in the industrial sector.

Why Pursue Operations Management?

Operations Management is an ideal career option for those students who are innovative and hold a good understanding of the development of various new processes and procedures. Let us throw some light on the benefits provided by an MBA degree in Operations Management-

1. Stay Up-to-Date with New & Emerging Technology

Individuals pursuing an MBA in Operations Management, can enhance their knowledge and skills as they will be working with innovative technology within a dynamic business environment. It will help them to overcome the challenges that they will be facing in the complex business environment.

2. Lucrative Job Opportunities

An MBA in Operations Management opens wide avenues for aspirants who have an interest in the Operations department of the organisation. Since the operations involved in the product evolution and development are connected with almost every department of the company, it is a great job opportunity for people aspiring to take up crucial roles in an organisation. 

3. High Chances of Gaining Managerial Roles

After securing an MBA degree in Operations Management, the aspirants have a high chance of securing managerial and decision-making roles in the organisation. This is because Operations Management deals with the administration of various business practices involved to achieve the highest level of operational efficiency in an organisation.

4. Industry-Oriented Curriculum

The main aim of Operations Management is to execute corporate planning and techniques in the real business environment. The curriculum of the course is designed such that it helps students develop required skills and knowledge that are a prerequisite to work in a complex business environment. 

Hence, an MBA in Operations Management is an ideal choice for students who want to pursue a career in production and planning. The department aims at assuring the success of the product and the service they offer in order to ensure the success of the organisation.

Why Pursue a Distance MBA in Operations Management?

Distance MBA has been gaining wide popularity around the globe due to the numerous benefits it offers. It has become a good way to continue education and move ahead in career for those students who are unable to pursue a full-time MBA course due to time and location constraints. Other than this, there are many other benefits offered by a Distance MBA in Operations Management. These are:

1. Cost Effectiveness

A Distance MBA course is quite affordable compared to a conventional MBA programme. A large chunk of expenses such as travel costs, hostel fee, accommodation expenses, etc. are eliminated while pursuing a Distance MBA programme which makes it quite economical for many of us.

2. In-Depth Learning

The curriculum provides you with a comprehensive learning and a detailed viewpoint of the practical aspects of running a business. The course offers an advanced industry-oriented curriculum that trains you to deal with all the unprecedented challenges which await you in the complex business world. 

3. Career Advancement Opportunities

After securing an MBA degree in Operations Management, an individual has a lot of job opportunities available to him. It guarantees lucrative packages and critical positions in top organisations. Thus, an MBA degree in Operations Management helps elevate the career of an individual.

FAQ’s

Q1. What is the eligibility criteria to pursue an MBA in Operations Management?

Ans. You need to have a Graduation degree from a recognised institute with a minimum aggregate of 50% or more. However, the percentage may vary from university to university. Some institutes also grant admission on the basis of entrance exams such as CAT, NMAT, XAT, SNAP, etc. 

Q2. What is an MBA in Operations Management?

Ans. MBA in Operations Management is a two-year postgraduate programme that imparts the required knowledge, training, and skills essential for the manufacturing of goods and services. 

Q3. What is the average salary package after an MBA degree in Operations Management?

Ans. The career opportunities for an MBA degree in Operations Management are many. The average salary for the same is around INR 7.12 LPA.
