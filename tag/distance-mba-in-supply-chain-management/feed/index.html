]]> Enrolling in an MBA programme in Supply Chain Management helps you acquire all the essential business competencies required to effectively evaluate, oversee, and enhance the performance of supply chains across various organisations.

This sought-after MBA programme also equips you with the proficiency to comprehend and evaluate the interdependence among the vital functional units of a company and the external entities that contribute to the development, manufacturing, and delivery of products.

The curriculum primarily focuses on imparting fundamental knowledge about transportation, logistics, and distribution management in supply chain operations, which apply to both private and public sectors. 

What is Supply Chain Management?

Supply Chain Management involves managing goods from their raw material stage to their final product stage. This process encompasses everything up until the delivery of the final product to the customer. To optimise their organisation’s supply chain, professionals in this field use various techniques such as forecasting, enhancing inventory and transportation efficiency, cost reduction, understanding the flow of goods, and aligning supply chain networks.

Why Pursue Supply Chain Management?

Regardless of their product range – cars, toys, aeroplane parts, t-shirts or toothpaste-all manufacturers rely on a supply chain to function. Effective management of supply chains can result in increased efficiency, reduced costs, improved management of supply and demand, and ultimately higher profits.

Furthermore, Supply Chain Management is a rapidly expanding field with a promising future. As the global supply chain continues to evolve and transform, the demand for professionals with expertise in supply chain management, strategy creation, execution and optimisation will only increase. 

Thus, there will be ample opportunities for individuals with relevant education and skills to pursue a fulfilling career in this field. Here are a few reasons why you should opt for an MBA in Supply Chain Management

• Increased Scope

The supply chain encompasses all aspects of a product’s life cycle, including acquisition, distribution, allocation, and delivery. The scope of this field is expanding rapidly due to technological advancements such as Blockchain and Cloud Computing.

As a Supply Chain Professional, your organisation will rely on you to predict market trends, ensure proper staffing, ensure timely delivery of parts, and react swiftly to unexpected issues. You will be an expert in prioritisation and can efficiently execute tasks, ultimately enabling others to perform their roles. 

The satisfaction that comes from being a driving force behind a team’s success can be incredibly fulfilling.

• Fast-Growing Industry

Despite the pandemic’s disruptions, supply chain careers have proven to be among the most resilient regarding salaries, job stability, and new opportunities. Moreover, as the global economy gradually recovers, there is an increased demand for raw materials, and warehousing requirements are shifting, making Logistics Management more complex. 

This underscores the need for highly skilled Supply Chain Professionals, particularly those who can optimise supply chain performance. As a result, there will be even more opportunities for individuals seeking employment in the Supply Chain industry.

• Improve Soft Skills

Soft skills are crucial in any profession but significant in Supply Chain Management. These skills are just as essential as the formal and technical knowledge required among Supply Chain Professionals, as their role requires them to interact with others extensively.

As a result, as you progress in your supply chain career, you will inevitably improve your soft skills. This will enhance your prospects for internal career advancement and make you a desirable candidate for hiring managers or recruiters should you decide to explore new career opportunities.

• Lucrative Careers

The demand for Supply Chain Professionals is expected to rise due to increasing opportunities in the field. As a result, businesses compete for highly skilled talent, leading to competitive salaries, particularly for leadership roles.

Despite being viewed by some as functional and mundane, supply chain careers can be lucrative. Supply Chain Professionals are well-rewarded, whether motivated by financial returns or career growth.

• Financially Rewarding

The average salary earned by a Supply Chain Manager is around 8,00,000 per annum, which can even hit the 80 Lakhs per annum mark depending on the location and the project you work on.

• Travel Abroad

Certain logistics positions involve frequent overseas travel, primarily work-related activities rather than tourism. Nonetheless, such trips provide numerous prospects for individuals aspiring to enter the international business arena.

Why Pursue a Distance MBA in Supply Chain Management?

Here’s why you should consider a distance MBA in Supply Chain Management.

• Flexibility

There are numerous benefits of pursuing an MBA through distance education, including flexibility. With this approach, you can study at your own pace and schedule without attending physical classrooms. Instead, you can participate in virtual classes from the comfort of your own home.

• Affordability

Compared to a regular MBA degree, pursuing an MBA through distance education is generally more cost-effective, meaning students can save significant money on their education.

• Study Resources

Enrolling in a distance MBA programme provides access to a wealth of study resources, including recorded lectures, eBooks, and audiobooks, supplied by your institute. In addition, you can attend virtual classes via video conferencing, allowing for a more immersive and interactive learning experience. These resources can facilitate in-depth study and learning.

• Convenience

A distance MBA offers a perfect alternative if you need more time to attend regular MBA classes because of work. It allows you the freedom to study at your own pace, offering you an easy way to learn while you earn.
